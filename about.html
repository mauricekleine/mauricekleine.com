About

I'm an AI engineer and indie maker based in Amsterdam. My background isn't a straight line—I started studying psychology, but switched to tech when I realized I wanted something more concrete and impactful. What began as a late start in coding turned into 14+ years of building software across startups and scale-ups.

While at university, I built a logistics system out of frustration with how data was handled at my part-time job. Six years later, I sold it—my first small but proud exit. From there, I wore many hats: frontend engineer, backend engineer, product owner, and engineering manager leading teams of developers.

In 2023, I founded Subthread where we built dozens of AI-powered products—from chatbots to recruitment pipelines to consumer apps. That experience showed me how powerful AI can be for businesses of all sizes, not just tech companies. Now I help SMEs unlock time and efficiency by building AI-powered automations that streamline internal workflows.

What I Build

I treat life as a series of experiments. The same applies to building products—I ship small internet products, learn from them, and keep iterating. Even difficult experiments are worth the effort as long as you keep learning.

Current projects:

  • Mockly - A tool for creating realistic chat mockups. Empowers marketers, creators, and filmmakers to generate conversations for 15+ messaging platforms.
  • ONESIXTYEIGHT - Helping people get more out of their 168 hours each week, starting with a functional mushroom blend for focus without the crash.
  • NonoBench - A benchmark for evaluating how well LLMs solve Nonogram puzzles. Interactive results, visualizations, and leaderboards for AI reasoning capabilities.
  • Chief - A CLI tool that orchestrates autonomous AI coding agents using the "Ralph Wiggum methodology"—a five-phase approach to AI-assisted development.

Community

I run Hackadam, a monthly meetup in Amsterdam for indie makers, coders, designers, and hardware hackers who build their own apps and startups. We meet, share what we're working on, have lunch together, do demos, and hang out. It's part of the broader HACKA* Network.

Building alone can be isolating. Having a community of people who understand the journey—the wins and the failures—makes the process more sustainable and enjoyable.

Philosophy

My main goal is to have the biggest comfort zone possible by continuously pushing boundaries. Growth comes from stepping outside your comfort zone, but you need a safe base to return to. Travel, building products, and community all expand that zone.

I try to observe without judging and extract what aligns with my values. Learning develops new perspectives, not just new information. Any experiment is worth the effort as long as you keep learning—just because an experiment didn't result in a happier life doesn't mean it failed.

The real value of technology lies in solving everyday problems for real people. That's what keeps me building.
